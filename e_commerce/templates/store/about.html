E-commerce, short for electronic commerce, encompasses the buying and selling of goods or services using electronic networks, primarily the internet.
Key Aspects:
  • Online Shopping: Consumers browse and purchase products or services through websites, mobile apps, or other digital platforms.
  • Beyond Retail: E-commerce extends beyond online shopping, including:
    • Business-to-Business (B2B) transactions: Companies conducting business with other companies online.
    • Online auctions and marketplaces: Platforms like eBay where individuals and businesses can buy and sell items.
    • Digital downloads: Purchasing music, movies, software, ebooks, etc.
    • Online banking and financial services: Managing accounts, making payments, and transferring funds electronically.
Benefits:
  • Convenience: Shop 24/7 from anywhere with internet access.
  • Wide Selection: Access to a global market with a vast array of products.
  • Price Comparison: Easily compare prices from multiple sellers.
  • Personalized Experiences: Tailored recommendations, targeted ads, and customized shopping journeys.
E-commerce has revolutionized how businesses operate and how consumers shop, offering unprecedented convenience and access to a global marketplace.
